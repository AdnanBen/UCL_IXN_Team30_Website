Project Background


VPE’s mission is to create more engaging environments for online communication, including educational online activities, but also casual social events. As students ourselves, we have witnessed firsthand the issues online learning can cause and how disruptive it can be. Spending all our time watching unengaging lectures makes us less focused and less driven. The same goes for online catch ups with friends too, it’s hard to pay attention when those you’re talking to are separate little boxes on a screen. These experiences can be very isolating. For these reasons, we hope the creation of a conferencing app that utilises 3D environments will hugely increase the appeal of online communication, and make it feel more like the in person interactions we miss so much.

Client Introduction


John McNamara is a Master Inventor at IBM, Honorary Professor, and currently leads IBM UK University Programs.

Dean Mohamedally is a Principal Teaching Fellow at UCL and is the module lead for Systems Engineering.

Project goals


Our main goal for this project was to create a desktop app that aims to make video calls more stimulating by giving them a more personal aspect. We wanted to produce a piece of conferencing software that could challenge the negative stereotype around online communication.


Specific goals:

  • A simple UI to guide someone through the choosing process of one of our various environments.

  • A configuration mechanism so users can configure their chosen scene with objects and different camera angles.

  • A saving mechanism so configurations can be stored and accessed at any time.

  • Realistic environments in regards to lighting and textures.

  • The ability to use a green screen or to use automatic background removal.

Requirement Gathering


Client Requirements:

In order to understand exactly what our clients expected from our finished project, we scheduled multiple meetings with both of them before making a start. We recorded everything regarding project requirements, and when there was a conflict between expectations we made sure to clear these up with both clients to ensure our team knew exactly what our deliverables were. We continuously kept close contact with both clients throughout the entire project to be sure we were producing something that was up to their standards.


User Requirements:

To obtain a comprehensive and concise representation of user requirements we conducted semi-structured interviews which allowed for a deeper dive into any interesting idea or comment. The first user we interviewed was a lecturer at an Irish university, and the second was a fellow student at UCL. We made sure to take any concerns or advice about the platform into consideration.


Lecturer Script

Do you find online lecturing is interactable and immersive enough as it is now?​
No, I feel my students quickly get bored when watching my lectures.​

Would you find software that could provide 3D environments for you to give your lectures in useful?​
Yes, I think that could make my lectures much more interesting.​

What kind of things would you like to be able to do / change in the environment?​
It would be great if I could have slides behind me like in a real lecture, and a whiteboard that I could do questions on. I'd also enjoy having a clock, or a lectern that had my name on it.​

What potential downfalls could you identify with this type of software?​
Honestly, the learning curve. If it's too complicated to use comfortably then the benefits may not outweigh the time lost fiddling around. It'd also have to work only on my laptop.


Student Script

How are you finding your online lectures this year?​​
They are okay but I miss the feeling of being on campus. The experience feels quite shallow and boring.​

What do you mean by "shallow"?​
I'm just staring at slides and videos​.

Would a customizable 3D lecturing environment improve your experience?​​
Being able to see the lecturer moving around would help improve the shallow aspect and the lecturer having customizable objects in the scene would be cool.

Like a projector screen displaying slides?​
Yes, absolutely.​

Would you utilize this software in different environments to showcase coursework's or perhaps in a social context?​​
Definitely, as long as it is reliable. I wouldn’t want to risk losing marks over bugs or waste the limited social time I have with friends trying to get stuff to work.

Personas and Scenarios / Use Cases

After our interviewing process, we constructed the following two personas so represent our main two user groups. We also wrote a short scenario / use case for each of them.
  • LEWIS HOWARD
  • ERIC DOYLE

Due to the current situation, Lewis is obligated to teach his lectures from home. This is new territory for him, and he has received criticism from students about how his online lectures are being taught – they are finding the lectures unstimulating as it is very difficult to become immersed. Lewis wants to change this, so he downloads our software.

The uncomplicated setup and steps are relatively easy for Lewis to follow, and he is soon ready to start filming lectures using the software. He uses a lecture hall background for his first class and enjoys interacting with the surrounding environment by displaying his slides on a screen behind him. He removes the desk in the scene so he is unobstructed. There are more advanced options he can now explore as he becomes more familiar with the software, like saving camera angles and adding more presenters. His students respond positively to this change of teaching styles.

Eric Doyle is a computer science student. Due to the pandemic he has been forced to engage in his usual learning and socialising activities through online meeting software. He can’t help but feel he is missing out on more exciting and interactive experiences. He finds current meeting software too simplistic and boring.

One day he joins an online lecture and sees his professor utilising a customisable environment where he can show slides behind him. He finds the lecture much more interesting and lifelike. After seeing our software in action, he is excited to download it to use when presenting his coursework. He is also excited to utilise the software using the meeting room environment for trivia night with his friends.

When he downloads the software he finds its minimalist design makes it easy to choose a new presenting environment. He uses one of our various scenes to give a presentation for one of his modules, and the markers appreciate how the environment helps immerse them in the content Eric covers.
